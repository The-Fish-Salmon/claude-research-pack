# Mode: `review`

Evaluate a draft document the user provides — a manuscript section, a literature review, a grant aim, an argument essay. The output is editorial feedback, not a new piece.

## Workflow

1. **Read the draft** in full. Identify the central claim(s).
2. **Extract every cited source** in the draft. Build a citation list.
3. **Investigation** — 1× `investigator.md` to verify each citation exists and says what the draft claims it says. Use Semantic Scholar; download abstracts; download full text only when the abstract is ambiguous.
4. **Bias auditor** — does the draft cherry-pick? What major contradicting work isn't cited?
5. **Devil's Advocate** — strongest counter-argument the draft does not address.
6. **Editorial pass** — `editor.md`. Clarity, structure, citation form, hedging accuracy.
7. **Hand-off** — `00_Inbox/review-{slug}-{date}.md`.

## Required sections

- Summary of the draft's argument (in your words, ≤200 words; user confirms accuracy)
- Citation audit:
  - Citations that check out
  - Citations that don't exist OR don't say what's claimed (each with the discrepancy)
- Missing-citations list (major works that should have been engaged with)
- Substantive critique:
  - Strongest counter-argument
  - Methodological concerns
  - Overstatement / hedging issues
- Editorial notes (clarity, structure)
- Provenance

## Tone

Be specific and useful, not adversarial. The goal is to make the draft stronger. Cite the specific paragraph/page when raising an issue.
